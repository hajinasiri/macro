Sustainable Finance Map

Introduction

The Sustainable Economy map was developed through a system innovation process which included a great deal of desk-research, interviews, workshops and a survey, mostly funded by the Tellus Mater foundation. The map provides a structural view of the economy that can be used to better direct our Gender lens investing efforts and drive important systemic change.

This map is built on this basic framework , on which we can plot:
the basic economic flows
the key actors and forces
plus additional details .

Layers

On top of this structural view of the economy, we can then overlay additional layers :

The different 'capitals' that support the production of goods and services.

A summary of the various risk categories that arise from ignoring long-term social and environmental externalities, and the many risks.

How supply & demand influence and limit each other across the economic chains.

Leverage Points

In 2014 Tellus Mater conducted a survey re the key leverage points for change in the finance system. Here we overlay those leverage points , and the organizations cited by the 80 survey respondents as doing good and interesting work against these leverage points. The number to the left of the organization's name indicates the number of mentions they received in the survey.


Finance Dialogue
Friends Provident Meeting

On 17 November, 2015 the Friends Provident Foundation brought together a number of organisations for a 'systems mapping seminar'. As part of that meeting the seminar participants positioned themselves on this financial systems map in the area where they are most active (noting that organizations work across a number of different map elements). You can learn more about these organizations by double-clicking on their logos, which will take you to their home page.
