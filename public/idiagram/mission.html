Mission



Idiagram’s mission is to develop tools and content to help people deal effectively with complex systemic problems. There are two essential features of system-level challenges that we seek to address: **1. Collective Understanding** – Understanding the many different factors of the problem and how they interconnect. By definition, systemic problems are composed of many different pieces dynamically interacting. This makes it inherently difficult to understand how the system(s) work, and exactly which interventions will shift the system in the desired direction. Note that we often have a great deal of information about how the system works, but we’re lacking is the tools and processes to integrate that diverse knowledge into a comprehensive view of the problem and the potential solutions. **2. Collective Action** –  Aligning the many different people who must act in concert to accelerate meaningful change. A key entailment of a systemic problem is that its many pieces are understood by, controlled by, are important to, many different people – often people with diverse viewpoints and conflicting interests. Thus understanding the problem is not enough; we must also align the many different stakeholders who need to work together to shift the system. We need a participatory process that enables the stakeholders to come to shared view of the problem, agree on a way forward, and coordinate their on-going actions. To address these twin challenges, Idiagram is committed developing the theories, process and tools for multi-stakeholder system change, and applying them to the important systemic problems we is face.
